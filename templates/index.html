General Knowledge

Stocks represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you're purchasing a share of that company, making you a part-owner. Stocks are traded on exchanges like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ, where their prices fluctuate based on supply and demand, company performance, and broader economic factors. Investors buy stocks hoping they will increase in value, allowing them to sell at a profit, or to earn dividends—payments made to shareholders from the company’s earnings. Stocks are generally seen as long-term investments and are a core component of many investment portfolios due to their potential for growth over time.

Cryptocurrencies, on the other hand, are digital or virtual currencies that use cryptography for security. Unlike stocks, which represent ownership in a company, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and often based on blockchain technology—a distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers. Bitcoin, created in 2009, was the first and is still the most well-known cryptocurrency, but thousands of others, like Ethereum and Ripple, have since emerged. Cryptocurrencies are highly volatile, with prices influenced by factors like market demand, technological advancements, regulatory news, and investor sentiment. Unlike traditional stocks, they are not tied to the performance of a particular company but to the perceived value of the underlying technology or utility.
